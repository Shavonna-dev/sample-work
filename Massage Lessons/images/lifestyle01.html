Lifestyle:

Excercise and fitness: Swimming, cycling, jogging, skiing, dancing, walking and dozens of other activities can help your heart.Exercise improves heart function, lowers blood pressure and blood cholesterol, helps manage diabetes, and helps control weight. 30 minutes 3 times a week.
Eat a healthy diet: Nutritious and well-balanced ,low in saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol, and salt, high in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
Maintain a healthy weight: Excess body fat leads to health problems such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol.

Having excess abdominal body fat is also a health risk. Men with a waist of more than 40 inches around and women with a waist of 35 inches or more are at risk for health problems.

To lose weight, you must eat less and move more. Your body needs to burn more calories than you take in.
Drink 8 to 10 glasses of water a day.

Avoid excessive alcohol: which can do a number on your liver.
Follow the food pyramid:
3-5 servings of vegetables a day.
2-4 servings of fruits a day.
2-3 servings of meat, fish, eggs, nuts, beans or legumes a day.
2-3 servings of dairy products a day.
6-11 servings of grains a day such as rice, bread, cereals, and pasta.
Occational use of fats, oils, and sweets.
